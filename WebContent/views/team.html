Dr Daniel Bradshaw

Senior Clinical Fellow, Public Health England

Dan Bradshaw is a consultant in the Virus Reference Department, National Infection Service, Public Health England. He trained as a sexual health physician before undertaking a post-CCT fellowship in virology at PHE, where he supported the clinical validation of the HCV whole genome sequencing pipeline. His research interests include HIV/HCV coinfection.

Dr Rob Gifford

Senior Research Fellow at the Centre for Virus Research

Dr Gifford leads a group whose research addresses the ecology and evolution of viruses. He has a strong interest in facilitating the use of virus sequence data resources in clinical, research and public health contexts. He directs the overall GLUE framework project.

Dr Tamyo Mbisa

Head of Antiviral Unit, Public Health England

Tamyo Mbisa is Head of the Antiviral Unit within the Virus Reference Department at Public Health England. His research interests include mechanisms of antiviral resistance, molecular epidemiology, virus-host interactions and the development of novel sequencing methods. He also co-ordinates the UK HIV Resistance Network of NHS laboratories undertaking genotypic antiviral resistance testing.

Professor John McLauchlan

Professor of Viral Hepatitis at the Centre for Virus Research

Prof McLauchlan leads a research group investigating viral hepatitis. He is also a co-Chair of HCV Research UK and a collaborator in the STOP-HCV consortium.

Dr Emma Thomson

Clinical Senior Lecturer at the Centre for Virus Research

Dr Thomson leads a research group investigating viral Hepatitis and the ecology and evolution of emerging virus infections. She holds a Wellcome Trust fellowship relating to T-cell mediated evolution of HCV during acute infection and is also a collaborator in the STOP-HCV consortium.

Dr Josh Singer

Research Software Engineer at the Centre for Virus Research

Dr Singer is the lead software engineer for GLUE, the data-centric virus sequence software system which underlies the HCV-GLUE project. He also maintains the HCV-GLUE dataset and web application.
